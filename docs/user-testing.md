# User-testing plan

No effectiveness or perceptual-equivalence claim is supported by the current prototype. Testing must begin only after sprite roles, metrics, and experimental bounds are frozen and preregistered.

## Affected-user study

- recruit participants by self-described or clinically known CVD type without storing unnecessary identity data;
- compare original and candidate palettes on representative gameplay backgrounds;
- include A/B preference, `both similar`, `both difficult`, undo, and fine-adjustment paths;
- measure role-identification errors, response time, confidence, fatigue, and preference;
- analyse Protan, Deutan, and Tritan separately;
- retain the right to reject every candidate.

## Normal-vision fairness review

- use blinded original/candidate comparisons;
- test whether candidates are detectably more salient or provide faster role recognition than the original baseline;
- predefine equivalence or non-inferiority bounds rather than interpreting a nonsignificant difference as equivalence;
- inspect multiple stages, effects, and backgrounds.

Publish the protocol, exclusions, uncertainty intervals, failures, and threshold changes. Do not convert exploratory results into scientific constants.
